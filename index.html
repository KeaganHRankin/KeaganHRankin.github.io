Research

I am a PhD student and Vanier Scholar at University of Toronto Engineering. My research (supervised by Shoshanna Saxe and Daniel Posen) investigates the material demands of cities and infrastructure systems. I am determining the amount of materials we need, how the demand for materials effects our energy systems, and how we can meet material demands without overstepping climate limits. Some examples of my research:

  • The Future Infrastructure Growth (FIG) model, a simulation tool we created that won a national research award from the Canadian Government.
  • A dataset we built using LCA and BIM tools to determine why some types of housing have lower material GHG emissions than others. This work helped inform changes to Toronto's housing policy.
I recently returned from a visting position at the University of Cambridge, where I was leading a project to determine the carbon budgets of thousands of cities across the world. I completed my BSc in Engineering at the University of New Brunswick in my hometown of Fredericton, NB.
